Reducing Tomato Wastage through Tomato Paste Production

Tomato wastage is a significant issue in many agricultural regions, including the Northern Region of Uganda. Despite the high nutritional value and economic potential of tomatoes, a substantial portion of the harvest is lost due to various factors. These losses not only affect the income of farmers but also contribute to food insecurity and environmental degradation.

This project aims to address high post-harvest losses, market fluctuations, and environmental concerns by converting fresh tomatoes into tomato paste, thus presenting a viable solution to reduce wastage and enhance the economic and nutritional value of tomatoes.

By addressing these issues, we can improve food security and promote sustainable agricultural practices in the region.
